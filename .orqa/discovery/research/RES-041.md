## Findings

### Scoring Dimension Inconsistency (CRITICAL)

Four different scoring dimension sets exist across epics:

| Set | Used By | Dimensions |
|-----|---------|-----------|
| A | [EPIC-001](EPIC-001) through [EPIC-024](EPIC-024) | pillar, impact, dependency, effort |
| B | [EPIC-032](EPIC-032), [EPIC-033](EPIC-033) | dogfood-value, foundation, user-visible, scope, dependency-risk |
| C | [EPIC-034](EPIC-034) through [EPIC-048](EPIC-048) | user-value, pillar-alignment, dependency-weight, effort, risk |
| D | [EPIC-049](EPIC-049) through [EPIC-053](EPIC-053) | dogfood-value, user-facing, foundation, complexity |

Cross-epic priority comparison is meaningless. The schema allows any scoring keys.

### DOC-NNN Phantom References (CRITICAL)

20+ epics reference DOC-NNN identifiers in `docs-required` and `docs-produced` fields. No DOC-NNN artifact files exist. These are unresolvable graph edges. The prompt-injector detects DOC-NNN patterns but cannot resolve them to files.
